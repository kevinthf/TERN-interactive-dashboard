## Data Source
TERN is a research team that measures key terrestrial ecosystem attributes in Australia over time and stores these valuable observations in its database.
By leveraging this rich and valuable resource, we can explore the data and uncover interesting patterns, trends, and insights from the raw datasets.

The datasets were acquired from the [TERN EcoPlots](https://ecoplots.tern.org.au/) platform, which hosts a wide range of environmental and ecological data. 

The detailed procedure for downloading raw data from TERN EcoPlots can be found [here](https://ternaus.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/TERNSup/pages/2330394627/TERN+EcoPlots+User+Guide).



## Data Cleaning
The Data_preprocessing R script mainly conducting data cleaning and summarizing to produce output csv file for dashboard presentation.

Here we use three datasets including "plant occurence", "weather" and "soil profile" to conduct following process and visualisation.

1. plant occurence- Dataset containing observations of plant species (identified by their scientific names) recorded within surveyed sites. <br/> We aggregate these observations by survey site and survey period to generate summaries of
  - species richness
  - taxom abundance (family, genus, species) 
2. weather- Dataset contianing weather attributes of surveyed sites
  - precipitation
  - solar radiation
  - temperature (max, min, mean)
3. soil profile- dataset contianing weather attribute of surveyed sites
  - soil pH
  - soil electric conductivity
  - soil texture

The three datasets are then joined to investigate the relationships and correlations between the variables.

If you are interested, you can also download other datasets from the [TERN EcoPlots](https://ecoplots.tern.org.au/) and modify the code to generate other datasets.<br/> Make sure they are saved in the rawdata folder to conduct the template data preprocessing.
